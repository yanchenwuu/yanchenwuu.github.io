About

I am a researcher working on numerical modeling of interfacial phenomena. Since Oct. 2023, I have worked as a postdoctoral researcher at MIT in the Department of Chemical Engineering, focusing on modeling lipid nanoparticle formation in a project supported by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration. Prior to that, I conducted doctoral and postdoctoral research at KIT on phase-field modeling, droplet wetting, evaporation, and interfacial phenomena.

I received my doctoral degree in Mechanical Engineering from the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology in 2021, where my dissertation was awarded the highest academic honor of Summa Cum Laude.

My research focuses on the physics and modeling of liquid interfaces, including droplet wetting, capillary transport, evaporation and condensation, nucleation, multiphase flow, and phase-field simulations. I am particularly interested in how surface geometry, wettability, and thermodynamic driving forces regulate interfacial processes in confined and structured environments.

Research Interests

  • Interfacial phenomena, soft matter physics, nucleation, and thermodynamics
  • Droplet wetting, evaporation, condensation, and capillary transport
  • Geometrically confined nucleation and phase transitions in micro-/nanoscale structures
  • Microfluidics, surface science, 3D printing, and intelligent manufacturing
  • Multiscale modeling: phase-field modeling, population balance modeling, molecular dynamics, and CFD
